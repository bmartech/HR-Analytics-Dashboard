# 📈 Business Insights

## Executive Summary

The HR Analytics Dashboard provides a comprehensive view of the organization's workforce, highlighting employee demographics, workforce composition, and attrition trends.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Workforce Composition

- The organization employs **1,470** people.
- The majority of employees are aged **25–34**.
- Research & Development is the largest department.

---

### 2. Attrition

- Overall attrition is approximately **16%**.
- Research & Development experiences the highest employee turnover.
- Sales Executives and Laboratory Technicians account for a significant number of employee exits.

---

### 3. Overtime

Employees who work overtime exhibit noticeably higher attrition than employees who do not.

---

### 4. Employee Satisfaction

Most employees report a **Good** work-life balance, although attrition remains concentrated within specific departments and job roles.

---

## Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

- Investigate the causes of high attrition in Research & Development.
- Review overtime policies to improve employee well-being.
- Develop retention initiatives for high-turnover job roles.
- Continue monitoring employee satisfaction and work-life balance.
- Use the dashboard regularly to support strategic workforce planning.

---

## Conclusion

This dashboard enables HR leaders to monitor workforce health, identify areas of concern, and make data-driven decisions that improve employee retention and organizational performance.
